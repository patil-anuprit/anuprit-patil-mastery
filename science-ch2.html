1. Introduction

Many substances around us have a sour taste, bitter taste, or soapy feel. Lemon juice is sour. Soap solution feels slippery. These properties help us classify substances into acids and bases.

Acid: A substance that produces Hydrogen ions (H⁺) in aqueous solution.

Base: A substance that produces Hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in aqueous solution.

4. Properties of Acids

  • Sour in taste
  • Turn blue litmus red
  • Produce H⁺ ions in water
  • React with metals
  • React with metal carbonates
  • Conduct electricity in aqueous form

Acids and Metals

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

Hydrogen gas is released.

5. Properties of Bases

  • Bitter in taste
  • Soapy to touch
  • Turn red litmus blue
  • Produce OH⁻ ions in water
NaOH → Na⁺ + OH⁻

6. Chemical Reactions of Acids

Acids + Metals

Acids react with metals to produce salt and hydrogen gas.

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

Acids + Metal Carbonates

Acids react with metal carbonates to produce salt, water and carbon dioxide.

CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂

Acids + Metal Bicarbonates

NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂

7. Chemical Reactions of Bases

Base + Acid

This reaction is called Neutralization.

NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O
Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water

8. Why Do Acids Show Acidic Behaviour?

Acids produce Hydrogen ions (H⁺) only in aqueous solution. Without water, acids cannot release H⁺ ions.

Example

HCl + H₂O → H⁺ + Cl⁻

Dry HCl gas does not show acidic properties.

9. Why Do Bases Show Basic Behaviour?

Bases produce Hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in water.

NaOH → Na⁺ + OH⁻

OH⁻ ions are responsible for basic nature.

10. Understanding pH Scale

pH is a scale used to measure acidity and basicity.

pH Value Nature
0 – 6 Acidic
7 Neutral
8 – 14 Basic
Lower pH = Stronger Acid Higher pH = Stronger Base

11. Importance of pH in Everyday Life

Human Body

Blood normally maintains pH around 7.4. Even small changes can be dangerous.

Digestive System

Stomach contains Hydrochloric Acid. Excess acid causes indigestion. Antacids help neutralize excess acid.

Tooth Decay

When mouth pH falls below 5.5, tooth enamel starts dissolving.

Soil pH

Plants grow best in suitable soil pH. Farmers use lime to reduce soil acidity.
