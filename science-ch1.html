Chemical Reactions and Equations

1. Introduction

Everything around us undergoes changes. Milk becomes curd. Iron rusts. Wood burns. Food gets digested. Many of these changes produce entirely new substances. Such changes are called Chemical Reactions.

A Chemical Reaction is a process in which one or more substances undergo change to form one or more new substances having different properties.

2. Characteristics of Chemical Reactions

1. Evolution of Gas

When zinc reacts with dilute hydrochloric acid, hydrogen gas is produced.

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

2. Change in Colour

Iron nail placed in copper sulphate solution changes the blue solution into green.

Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

3. Change in Temperature

Many reactions release heat. Such reactions are called Exothermic Reactions.

4. Formation of Precipitate

An insoluble solid formed during reaction is called a precipitate.

Pb(NO₃)₂ + 2KI → PbI₂↓ + 2KNO₃

3. Chemical Equations

A Chemical Equation is the symbolic representation of a chemical reaction.

Word Equation

Magnesium + Oxygen → Magnesium Oxide

Chemical Equation

Mg + O₂ → MgO

This equation is not balanced. Therefore it does not satisfy the Law of Conservation of Mass.

4. Balanced Chemical Equations

According to the Law of Conservation of Mass, Mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction. Therefore the number of atoms of each element must be equal on both sides.

Unbalanced Equation

Mg + O₂ → MgO

Balanced Equation

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO
Board Exam Tip: Never change chemical formulas while balancing. Only coefficients can be changed.

5. Types of Chemical Reactions

A. Combination Reaction

Two or more substances combine to form a single product.

CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂

Quicklime + Water → Slaked Lime

Daily Life Example

Burning of Magnesium Ribbon

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

B. Decomposition Reaction

One compound breaks down into simpler substances.

CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

This reaction occurs on heating.

Types of Decomposition

Type Cause Example
Thermal Heat CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
Electrolytic Electricity 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
Photolytic Sunlight 2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂

C. Displacement Reaction

A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.

Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

Iron is more reactive than Copper. Therefore Iron displaces Copper.

D. Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions occurs between compounds.

Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄ + 2NaCl

A white precipitate of Barium Sulphate is formed.

6. Oxidation and Reduction

Oxidation

Addition of Oxygen OR Removal of Hydrogen

2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO

Reduction

Removal of Oxygen OR Addition of Hydrogen

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O

Redox Reaction

When oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously.

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O
Important Board Definition: A reaction in which oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously is called a Redox Reaction.

7. Effects of Oxidation in Daily Life

Corrosion

The gradual destruction of metals due to reaction with air, moisture and chemicals.

Examples

  • Rusting of Iron
  • Black coating on Silver
  • Green coating on Copper

Rusting

Iron + Oxygen + Moisture → Rust

Prevention of Corrosion

  • Painting
  • Greasing
  • Galvanization
  • Electroplating
  • Alloy Formation

8. Rancidity

Oxidation of fats and oils leading to unpleasant smell and taste.

Examples

  • Chips becoming stale
  • Butter developing bad smell
  • Oil spoiling after long storage

Methods to Prevent Rancidity

  • Adding antioxidants
  • Nitrogen flushing
  • Refrigeration
  • Airtight containers

9. NCERT Important Questions

Q1. Why should a magnesium ribbon be cleaned before burning?

Magnesium ribbon develops a layer of magnesium oxide on its surface when exposed to air. This layer prevents proper burning. Therefore it is cleaned before burning.

Q2. What is a balanced chemical equation?

A chemical equation in which the number of atoms of each element is equal on both sides.

Q3. What is a decomposition reaction?

A reaction in which a compound breaks down into simpler substances.

10. Important Board PYQs

PYQ 1

Identify the type of reaction:

2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂

Answer: Decomposition Reaction

PYQ 2

Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

Answer: Displacement Reaction

PYQ 3

2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂

Answer: Photochemical Decomposition Reaction

11. Assertion Reason Questions

Assertion: Rusting is an oxidation reaction.

Reason: Iron combines with oxygen and moisture.

Answer: Both Assertion and Reason are true and Reason correctly explains Assertion.

Assertion: All decomposition reactions require heat.

Reason: Some decomposition reactions occur due to electricity and sunlight.

Answer: Assertion is false but Reason is true.

12. HOTS Questions

Question 1

Why are food packets filled with nitrogen gas?

Answer: Nitrogen prevents oxidation and therefore prevents rancidity.

Question 2

Why is galvanization preferred for iron?

Answer: It prevents corrosion by forming a protective zinc layer.

16. Complete Chapter Summary

Chemical reactions result in formation of new substances. Chemical equations represent reactions symbolically. Balanced equations obey the law of conservation of mass. Chemical reactions are classified as combination, decomposition, displacement and double displacement reactions. Oxidation involves addition of oxygen whereas reduction involves removal of oxygen. Corrosion damages metals while rancidity spoils food materials. Understanding these concepts is essential for CBSE Board examinations.
