1. Introduction

1. Introduction to Real Numbers

Real Numbers form the foundation of Mathematics. Every number that can be located on a number line is called a Real Number. Real numbers are used in counting, measurement, algebra, geometry, science, engineering, economics and almost every branch of mathematics.

The chapter Real Numbers helps students understand the structure of the number system, properties of numbers, methods for finding HCF, prime factorization, irrational numbers and decimal expansions.

What are Real Numbers?

A Real Number is any number that can be represented on the number line.

Examples:

  • 2
  • -7
  • 0
  • 3/5
  • 0.125
  • √2
  • √5
  • π

Classification of Numbers

The number system develops in stages:

  • Natural Numbers (N) = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 ...
  • Whole Numbers (W) = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 ...
  • Integers (Z) = ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3 ...
  • Rational Numbers (Q) = Numbers of the form p/q where q ≠ 0
  • Irrational Numbers = Numbers that cannot be written as p/q
  • Real Numbers (R) = Rational Numbers + Irrational Numbers

Important Relationship

Natural Numbers ⊂ Whole Numbers ⊂ Integers ⊂ Rational Numbers ⊂ Real Numbers

Examples of Rational Numbers

  • 1/2
  • 3/4
  • -8
  • 0
  • 2.75
  • 0.333...

Every terminating decimal and every recurring decimal is a rational number.

Examples of Irrational Numbers

  • √2
  • √3
  • √5
  • π
  • √11

Irrational numbers have non-terminating and non-repeating decimal expansions.

CBSE Important Facts

  • Every integer is a rational number.
  • Every whole number is an integer.
  • Every natural number is a whole number.
  • Every rational number is a real number.
  • Every irrational number is a real number.
  • Not every real number is rational.

Exam Tip

CBSE frequently asks questions based on classification of numbers, rational versus irrational numbers and decimal representations. These are among the highest-frequency concepts of this chapter.

2. Euclid's Division Lemma

Euclid's Division Lemma is a mathematical statement that helps us divide one positive integer by another positive integer. It states that for any two positive integers a and b, there exist unique whole numbers q and r such that: a = bq + r where: Dividend = a Divisor = b Quotient = q Remainder = r and 0 ≤ r < b

Example

When 29 is divided by 5:

29 = 5 × 5 + 4

Dividend = 29
Divisor = 5
Quotient = 5
Remainder = 4
